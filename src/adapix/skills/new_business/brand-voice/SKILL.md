# Brand voice

A new business needs to sound like one specific thing, not like every
business. The founder hasn't written enough copy yet to discover their
voice by accident, so your job is to declare it upfront in a way that
holds up across SMS, email, website, and in-person.

## Step 1 — Three anchor adjectives

Ask the founder to pick THREE adjectives that describe the voice. Push
them past obvious words. Examples to riff on:

- *Warm but not cute* — friendly without being precious
- *Direct but not blunt* — gets to the point without being cold
- *Confident but not boastful* — knows what it knows
- *Playful but not joking* — has wit without telling jokes
- *Calm but not bored* — present without being aggressive
- *Premium but not stuffy* — quality without snobbery

Reject single-word answers like "professional" or "fun" — they don't
encode anything actionable.

## Step 2 — A do-list and don't-list

Convert the three adjectives into specific writing rules. Examples:

**Do:**
- Use contractions ("we're", "you'll", "that's")
- Address one customer, not a crowd ("you", never "our valued customers")
- Lead with the verb in instructions ("Click reschedule", not "You can click...")
- Use specific times ("Tuesday at 3pm"), not vague ones ("soon")

**Don't:**
- Use jargon from a different industry
- Start with "Just" ("just wanted to follow up...")
- Use exclamation points unless something is actually exciting
- Apologize for taking up someone's time

The exact rules depend on the adjectives. Generate at least 5 do's and
5 don'ts that match.

## Step 3 — Three sample messages

Write three short messages in the voice, each ~40 words:

1. A reply to a customer asking about price
2. A booking confirmation
3. A response to a customer who's upset

Show the founder all three. Ask: *"Does this sound like you, or like
someone you don't want to be?"*

If it doesn't sound right, revise the adjectives — don't keep generating
in the wrong voice.

## Output — a voice doc

End with a single block the founder can save:

```
VOICE OF {business name}

Three anchors:
  1. ...
  2. ...
  3. ...

Do:
  - ...

Don't:
  - ...

Sample messages:
  ...
```

This is the doc they paste into any future tool, hand to any contractor,
or use to onboard their first hire.
