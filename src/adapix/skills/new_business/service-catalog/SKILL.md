# Service catalog

The founder needs a concrete list of what they're selling. Vague offers
("we do consulting") never close. Specific offers ("90-minute brand audit
delivered as a one-page memo, $400") close fast. Your job is to turn fog
into a list.

## Step 1 — Find out what they already know

Ask: *"In one or two sentences, who's your customer and what's the
single thing they pay you to do?"*

Don't accept business-school answers ("provide best-in-class solutions").
Push back until you have a real customer and a real outcome they're
paying for.

## Step 2 — Draft 3 to 5 distinct offerings

Each one is its own row with these fields:

- **Name** — 1-4 words, plain English, names a thing not a category
- **What it is** — one sentence the customer would understand
- **Who it's for** — one sentence about the customer who needs it
- **Scope** — what's included, with three concrete items
- **Delivery** — a session? a deliverable? a subscription? on-site? remote?
- **Rough time** — how long it takes to deliver one

Example for a hypothetical brand-strategy consultant:

```
1. Brand audit
   What it is: A 90-minute review of your existing brand with a written
   memo on what to fix first.
   Who it's for: Founders 1-3 years in who feel "off-brand" but can't
   name why.
   Scope: 1) audit of homepage + 3 social channels, 2) competitive scan,
   3) one-page memo with 5 prioritized fixes.
   Delivery: Async — you fill out a form, get the memo back in 5 days.
   Rough time: ~6 hours of your time per audit.
```

## Step 3 — Pick the lead offering

Of the 3-5, ask: *"Which one would you sell first if you could only sell
one?"* That's the lead offering — most marketing should point at it.

## Anti-patterns to push back on

- "Everything" / "anything they need" → too broad, no one buys it
- A long ladder of tiers (silver/gold/platinum) before there's any single
  thing that sells. Start with one. Ladder later.
- Offerings that overlap (two products that solve the same problem) —
  consolidate them.

## Output format

End the conversation by saving the catalog as a clean numbered list in
the chat. The user should be able to copy it into a doc and use it as-is.
