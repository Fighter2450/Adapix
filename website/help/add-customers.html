Add your customers

~2 minutes

Open Contacts. Two ways in:

  • Import a CSV — export from your spreadsheet or old CRM; Adapix maps names, phones, and emails.
  • Add one at a time — name, phone or email, and where they are with you (asked for a quote, missed an appointment, due for a visit).

The moment a customer is added, Adapix starts working: the first follow-up drafts immediately and waits in your Inbox for approval.

Consent matters: only add people who gave your business their contact info. That's both the right thing and a legal requirement for texting. See how opt-in works.

What happens when you import

  • You choose whether to chase. A checkbox on the import asks if Adapix should start following up with these contacts, or just store them until you say so.
  • First messages are rationed. Even with 200 imports, Adapix drafts at most 15 first messages a day, starting with whoever's been quiet longest. No flooded inbox, no spam-blast to your whole list.
  • Re-imports don't duplicate. Contacts are matched on phone number (and your ID column if you have one) — importing the same file twice skips everyone who already exists.
  • The notes column is gold. "Quoted $1,850 for water heater July 7, worried about cost" turns into a follow-up that names the job and the concern — the difference between a text that reads like a person and one that reads like spam.

Adapix remembers your customers

Every conversation teaches Adapix something about that specific person — a preference, a detail about their job, a concern they raised. It's remembered and used naturally in later conversations, so customers never have to repeat themselves.

  • Contacts with something learned show a 🧠 badge next to their name in the list.
  • Open a contact and expand the edit panel, or open their conversation, to see exactly what's been learned — each fact, with when it was learned.
  • Anything wrong or outdated can be deleted with one click.

See the whole conversation in one place

Click any contact to open their full history — every text, email, and call, in order, in a single view. No more piecing together what was said across different channels.
