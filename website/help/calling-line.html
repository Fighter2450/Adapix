Set up your calling line

~1 minute

Your business gets its own dedicated calling number — the same one every time, so customers learn it. Home screen → "Set up your calling number" bubble (or Settings → Messaging & channels) → one click provisions it, instantly, for free.

After every call Adapix places, you get the full recording and a written transcript in the Calls tab — you always know exactly what was said.

New number sounding "Spam Likely"? That's normal for the first few days — brand-new numbers have no caller reputation yet. Free registration at freecallerregistry.com clears it up fast.

Free number vs. the paid upgrade — what's actually different

Every business gets its own number automatically, at no cost — it's never shared with any other business on Adapix. The free version comes from a shared pool of ready-to-use numbers, which is why it's instant. Phone carriers trust a free, unregistered number less, though, so it's a little more likely to get flagged "Spam Likely" than a number registered to your business.

In Settings → Messaging & channels, you can upgrade to a real, carrier-purchased number for $1.50/month (cancel anytime). Same idea — still exclusively yours — just bought through an actual telecom carrier instead of borrowed for free, which carriers trust more and flag as spam less often. You'll see the exact cost on screen and have to confirm before anything is purchased.

Coming soon: getting your business's actual name (not just a number) to show on a customer's caller ID screen. That's a separate step on top of the paid upgrade — it requires submitting real business details for carrier verification, and typically takes a few business days to show up, the same as it would with any provider.

Queuing and placing a call

In the Calls tab, pick a contact and set what you want the call to accomplish — choose one of the built-in goals (quote a job, close a sale, book an appointment, check in, win back a lapsed customer) or select Describe your own to type something specific.

  • Queue call — waits for your approval like a text or email draft.
  • Call now — places the call immediately, skipping the queue.
  • Call at — pick a future date and time; Adapix places the call then automatically. Scheduled calls show up in their own section until they go out, and you can call now or cancel at any point before that.

If a call fails, the Calls tab shows the real reason why (busy line, no answer, carrier block, etc.) instead of a generic error — right there next to the attempt.
