# Adapix Marketing Playbook

**This is the source of truth for anything marketable.** Before writing website
copy, ads, outreach messages, social posts, the demo video script, or a
one-pager: read this file and market THESE things, in THIS order, under THESE
rules. If a marketing idea isn't grounded in something below, check with Rocco
before shipping it.

Last updated: July 14, 2026 · Owner: Ben (CMO) · Approver: Rocco (Founder & CEO)

---

## Who we're selling to

The owner of a small service business — plumber, contractor, salon, dentist,
detailer — who personally quotes jobs and personally loses the follow-up game.
They have 50–500 customers in a spreadsheet or a CRM they hate, they know
quotes go quiet, and they've been burned by software that overpromised. They
read texts between jobs. They do not care about AI; they care about money they
already earned almost getting away.

## The one-sentence pitch

**Adapix wins back the customers who went quiet — it writes the follow-up
texts, emails, and calls for you, and nothing sends until you say OK.**

---

## The hooks — market these, in this order

### 1. "Won back $4,200 this month" — the money counter
The dashboard shows the running total of jobs recovered, in dollars. This is
the hero of every asset: first 10 seconds of the demo video, top of the
homepage once we have a real screenshot, the line in every outreach message.
Owners don't buy AI — they buy visible dollars they'd written off.
- Demo framing: "What would this number be for your shop?"
- Renewal framing: "That number versus $99. That's the whole decision."

### 2. "Bring me 5 dead quotes" — the live demo moment
The strongest sales motion we have costs nothing: have the owner bring five
quotes that went quiet. Import them live. Watch Adapix draft five personal
messages naming THEIR actual jobs and prices — in front of them, in under two
minutes. When one of those dead quotes replies the next day, the deal closes
itself. Every demo, every video, every event: this is the script.

### 3. AI phone calls from their own number
Nobody gives a solo plumber an AI that CALLS customers for $99 — the $400/mo
incumbents don't do it. This is the "wait, it does WHAT?" moment. Always show
it, never bury it. Pair with: transcripts land in the dashboard, and the owner
approves the call plan before any call is placed.
Every business gets this dedicated number free and instantly at signup — never
shared with anyone else on Adapix. There's also an optional $1.50/mo upgrade
to a real carrier-purchased number (vs. the free default), which cuts down on
"Spam Likely" flagging. Don't oversell the upgrade — it's a nice-to-have
add-on, not the headline; the free dedicated number is what closes the "wait,
it does WHAT?" moment on its own.

### 4. "Nothing sends without your OK" — the trust moat
Every draft waits in the Inbox. The owner reads it, edits it (click the text,
change anything), then sends. This closes the skeptic and it's the exact
opposite of every "the bot texted my customers something embarrassing" horror
story. Competitors automate; we supervise. Say it plainly, everywhere.
Supporting details: anti-spam voice (messages open "Hi Maria, it's Rocco from
Rocco's Plumbing" and name the actual job and price — never "just checking
in"), 15-first-messages-a-day ration, replies pause the sequence, STOP always
honored.

### 5. It doesn't just send a text — it holds the conversation
When a customer replies, Adapix reads it, answers real questions using what
the owner taught it (services, pricing, hours, FAQs), and keeps talking —
not a one-and-done blast. If something needs a person, it's flagged in the
Inbox with the whole back-and-forth attached, not just the one message. The
owner can open any contact and see the full conversation in one place —
every text, email, and call, in order. Frame it as the difference between a
robocall and someone who actually answers: "Your customer texts back, and
Adapix texts back."

### 6. It remembers your customers, not just their name and number
Every conversation teaches Adapix something about the person on the other
end — the dog that needs to be locked up before a tech shows, that they're
only free after 5pm, that they mentioned a cheaper quote elsewhere. Adapix
remembers it and quietly uses it next time — nobody has to dig through old
texts or make the customer repeat themselves. The owner can see exactly
what's been learned about each contact, right on their profile, and delete
anything wrong. Frame it as the opposite of a form-letter follow-up system:
"It doesn't just remember what they bought — it remembers who they are."

### 7. The founding deal — $49/mo locked for a year, 10 spots
Code FOUNDING at checkout (it's a real Stripe promo, max 10 redemptions).
Framing: "One recovered job pays for the whole year." Always state spots
remaining honestly. When the 10 are gone, this hook retires — don't fake
scarcity.

### 8. The math — "one job pays for months of it"
$99/month, everything included, no tiers, no usage fees, 14-day free trial
(card up front, $0 charged today — NEVER claim "no card," that changed
7/16). A $1,500 water heater job recovered = 15 months of Adapix. Use the
customer's OWN numbers whenever possible (their average job value), not ours.

## Hard rules (non-negotiable)

1. **Never invent numbers.** No made-up testimonials, no fabricated stats, no
   "3.2× more bookings." We have zero customers until we don't. The ONLY
   numbers allowed are: real product facts ($99, 14 days, 15/day ration, 10
   founding spots), real customer results once they exist and the customer
   approved being quoted, and clearly-hypothetical math ("a $1,500 job would
   cover 15 months").
2. **Real product only.** If a feature isn't live at app.adapixai.com, it
   doesn't go in marketing. Check with Rocco if unsure.
3. **Business language, never dental.** Customers, jobs, quotes, owners —
   never patients, consults, treatment, practices.
4. **The voice is a straight-talking tradesperson's friend.** Short sentences.
   Contractions. No "leverage," "seamless," "revolutionize," "unlock." Read it
   aloud — if a plumber wouldn't say it, cut it.
5. **All signup links point to `https://app.adapixai.com/signup`.** Never the
   old Railway URL.
6. **Rocco is Founder & CEO. Ben is CMO.** Never "co-founders."
7. **Honesty is the brand.** We removed a fake testimonial once; it never
   comes back. When we're small, say we're small: "You'll talk to the founder"
   is a feature.

## Proof assets — build in this order

1. **60–90s demo video** (slot already exists on /how): open on the "Won back
   $X" counter → the 5-dead-quotes import → a draft that names a real job →
   tap Send → reply comes in. Real dashboard, seeded demo business, no stock
   footage.
2. **2–3 real dashboard screenshots** for the homepage (Inbox with drafts, the
   money counter, a conversation).
3. **One-page PDF** for local outreach: the pitch, the math, the founding
   offer, QR code to /pricing.
4. **First real customer quote** — the moment a founding customer says
   something true and good, ask permission and replace hypotheticals with it.
